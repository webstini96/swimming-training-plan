Your Swimming Progression

You can swim, but one 50m length leaves you gassed. Pool fitness is almost completely separate from any other sport. The goal of weeks 1–6 is simply to stop fighting the water. Everything else comes after.

Phase 1 — Just Survive

Weeks 1–2. Accumulate water time without panic or exhaustion. Rest as long as you need between lengths. No targets, no pace, no pressure.

Goal: 8–10 lengths per session with full rest between each

Knee-Safe Training

🦵 Two-beat kick only

One gentle kick per arm stroke. Reduces knee stress and conserves energy — what endurance swimmers actually use.

🚫 No deep knee flexion

Leg press stops at 70°. No lunges, full squats, or box jumps. Incompatible with both knees.

🏊 Breaststroke banned

Whip kick creates valgus torque on the right knee. Freestyle and backstroke only.

✅ Posterior chain first

Glutes and hamstrings protect both knees. Hip thrusts, leg curls, cable pull-throughs are your vocabulary.

🚲 Cycling is fine

Flat terrain, high seat, low resistance. Genuinely knee-friendly — good low-impact LISS.

⚡ Sharp = stop

Aching through effort is normal. Sharpness, clicking with pain, or instability: stop immediately.

Swimming Technique

💨 Exhale underwater

Exhale fully through nose/mouth while face is down, so your inhale when you turn is instant — not frantic.

🐢 Slower = easier

Fast frantic swimming kills technique. Slow down until breathing is controlled, then gradually build pace.

💪 High elbow catch

Keep elbow high on entry, pull with forearm and hand together. This is where most propulsion comes from.

📏 Long strokes

Reach fully forward on entry, pull all the way to your hip. Shorter strokes = more effort, less distance.
